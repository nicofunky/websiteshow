In October 1998, Kinoko Nasu releases five chapters of the novel series "The Garden Of Sinners" onto the intenet. He later released the two final chapters in August 1999. The novels sold as expected at first. Nasu's close friend, Takashi Takeuchi, sees potential in Nasu's writing and helps him set up Type-Moon to try to get his work out to a greater audience.

In 2001, they decied to bundle the novels with a different product they were selling at the time, Tsukihime. Since Tsukihime was a major hit for Type-Moon, the novel gained a significant following. Then on the 6th of August, 2004, the series was released comercially.

It was another massive hit. The novel sold over 700,000 copies.

Then, during Type-Moon's festival in December 2006, it was announced that Studio Ufotable were addapting the 7 released volumes of the novels as a movie series. The movies would later premire between the years of 2007 and 2009. A recap movie was also released before the 7th movie.
"Recalled-out Summer" would be released in September 2011.

These movies launched the popularity of the seires even higher than before. It even attracted some mainstream attention. The blu-ray and dvd releases of the movies also saw sucess.
