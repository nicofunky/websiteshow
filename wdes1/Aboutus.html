About us

Who are we?

We are registered charity based in TU Dublin that’s made up of students, past students and core staff, all of which are volunteers.
Our charity was set up in December 2014 with a view to create 3D printing prosthetic hands and arms for people who need them.

To date we have manufactured over 60 prosthetic hands and arms for recipients in Ireland, the UK and Africa.
We also make assistive devices such as modified joysticks for wheelchairs, heated joystick covers for wheelchairs and educational devices for the visually impaired.

Finally, we are the Irish chapter of the worldwide E-Nable organisation.

What do we do?

We make use of 3D modeling to create the prosthetics.
Our production only uses €10 of material, takes 20 hours to print and requires 2 hours to assemble for each device, with no cost to the recipients.

We also engage with our local community, like schools, hospitals and clubs. Some specific ones to name would be LauraLynn Children’s Hospice, Irish Amputee Football Association, and Childvision.

But in the end, we mainly aid children. That’s what our charity is all about.
